# Spain — Player Profiles

## Pedri (MF)
- **Role**: Central midfielder. Spain's pressing and possession engine.
- **WC2026 stats**: 11 interceptions (dataset record for any individual), +2.26 On-Off, 1 yellow card
- **Key insight**: Pedri's 11 interceptions is not just a stat — it reflects Spain's entire press working through him. He wins the ball and immediately recycles into possession. **1 yellow card entering the knockout round = one more = suspension. La Fuente may protect him early in matches.**

## Lamine Yamal (FW)
- **Role**: Right wing. Spain's most dangerous creator despite being 18.
- **WC2026 stats**: 9.69 shots/90 (the highest rate in Spain's squad), +3.37 On-Off, 7 shots, 1G, 13 crosses
- **Key insight**: Yamal cuts inside onto his left foot. His shots/90 rate is elite — he takes risks, gets into positions. Spain's best chance creator by volume and quality.

## Mikel Oyarzabal (FW)
- **Role**: Primary striker.
- **WC2026 stats**: 2G/10sh, the squad's leading scorer
- **Key insight**: Oyarzabal's goals came from the positions Spain creates — penalty area, clinical finishing. He doesn't generate his own chances; he converts what the system creates.

## Rodri (MF)
- **Role**: Defensive pivot. The structural foundation of Spain's midfield.
- **WC2026 stats**: 176/180 mins played — almost never subbed
- **Key insight**: Rodri screens in front of the CB pair. Without him, Spain's defensive compactness degrades. He played through injury concern — if he misses any R32 match, Spain's defensive profile changes significantly.

## Ferran Torres (FW) — Selection concern
- **WC2026 stats**: -4.19 On-Off — Spain measurably worse when he starts
- **Key insight**: Torres is a selection error when he starts. Baena (+1.45) and Olmo are the correct rotation options. La Fuente's persistence with Torres is the only clear tactical mistake in an otherwise excellent squad.
