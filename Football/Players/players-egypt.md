# Egypt — Player Profiles

## Mohamed Salah (FW/AM)
- **Role**: Captain, right wing / secondary striker. Egypt's creative hub and primary crossing outlet.
- **WC2026 stats**: G/SoT 0.33, 7 crosses/game, +1.13 On-Off
- **Shot profile**: High volume, moderate quality. Cuts in from right, rarely gets into the box centrally.
- **Key insight**: Salah's crossing creates Trézéguet's scoring opportunities. When Salah plays, Trézéguet must start — that combination is Egypt's only proven goal-creation mechanism.
- **Discipline**: 0 yellow cards through MD3

## Omar Marmoush (FW)
- **Role**: Starting striker, MD1-MD2. Selection error confirmed by data.
- **WC2026 stats**: 0G/8sh, -5.45 On-Off — the largest negative On-Off in the entire dataset
- **Key insight**: Every minute Marmoush plays, Egypt measurably underperform. He takes 8 shots and scores 0. He is not a bad player (Bundesliga pedigree) but tactically wrong for this system.
- **Verdict**: Should not start. Impact sub role only.

## Trézéguet (FW)
- **Role**: Impact substitute. Egypt's most clinical finisher.
- **WC2026 stats**: +5.45 On-Off, G/SoT 1.00 in ~15 mins, best impact sub in the dataset
- **Key insight**: Trézéguet scored the moment he entered. The On-Off differential vs Marmoush (+5.45 vs -5.45 = 10.9pt swing) is the clearest selection signal in the entire dataset. If Egypt start him, they are immediately a better team.

## Hamdy Fathy (DF)
- **Role**: Right back. Egypt's most exposed defensive position.
- **WC2026 stats**: -5.80 On-Off — the worst defensive On-Off in the dataset
- **Key insight**: When Fathy plays, Egypt concede significantly more. Opponents target his flank. If Egypt face a left-footed wide forward or inverted winger, Fathy is the attack route.
