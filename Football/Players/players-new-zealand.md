# New Zealand — Player Profiles

## Elijah Just (MF)
- **Role**: Central midfielder. NZ's most clinical player relative to opportunities.
- **WC2026 stats**: 2G, G/SoT 0.67 — the best conversion rate among NZ outfield players
- **Key insight**: Just scored twice from 3 shots — an elite conversion rate for a midfielder. He gets into goal-scoring positions despite NZ's limited attacking system. If NZ create chances, he finishes them.

## Sarpreet Singh (MF)
- **Role**: Attacking midfielder / wide player. NZ's most impactful player by On-Off.
- **WC2026 stats**: +5.08 On-Off across 164 minutes — the highest of any NZ player
- **Key insight**: NZ are measurably better with Singh on the pitch. He creates movement and combinations that NZ's otherwise direct style lacks.

## Stefan Crocombe (GK)
- **Role**: Starting GK. NZ's biggest vulnerability.
- **WC2026 stats**: 54.5% save% — 2nd worst in the dataset
- **Key insight**: Crocombe concedes 45.5% of shots on target. Against Belgium's xG regression in MD3, this produced 5 goals. Any team with quality shooting will score freely against NZ — GK quality makes them structurally unable to defend a lead.
