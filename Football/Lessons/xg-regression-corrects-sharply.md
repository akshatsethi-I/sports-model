# xG Regression Corrects in a Single Game

## The Lesson
When a team has a large accumulated xG deficit (goals scored vs xG), the correction often arrives in a **single explosive game** rather than gradually. Do not wait for gradual regression — back the regressing team in their most favorable structural matchup.

## Evidence

### Belgium WC2026
- After MD2: Belgium xG 3.14, Goals 1 (via OG), deficit = **-2.14** — the largest in the dataset
- MD3 opponent: New Zealand (Crocombe 54.5% save% — 2nd worst GK in dataset)
- MD3 result: **Belgium 5-1 New Zealand** — regression arrived all at once
- Prediction: Belgium to win ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐, Belgium 2+ goals ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — both hit

### Why it corrects in one game
- Shot quality hadn't degraded — Belgium's -2.14 was finishing, not chance creation
- The opponent's GK was the weakest Belgium faced — optimal conditions for correction
- Belgium's attack was unchanged — the same players who missed suddenly converted

## Confidence
**High** — backed by pre-existing regression-to-mean theory and confirmed empirically in WC2026.

## How to Apply
1. Identify teams with xG deficit > 1.50 after 2+ games
2. Check if the next opponent has a weak GK (save% < 70%)
3. If both conditions are true: back the regressing team to score 2+ goals
4. Quantify using the GK-adjusted SoT model.

## Counterexamples
- A team with a large xG deficit due to **poor shot quality** (not bad luck) will not regress to goals — they need to improve their chance creation first, not just their finishing
- If the next opponent has an elite GK (Beiranvand 86.7%), regression is suppressed
