# Cards Market

## When the Market Performs Well

### Reliable indicators (back cards)
1. **High-yellow referee** (avg > 4.0): The single most reliable indicator. Referee style overrides match context in most cases.
2. **Must-win team with players already booked**: If a team needs a win AND has starters on 1 yellow, they will foul urgently with suspension at risk. Cards follow.
3. **Physical mismatch**: A technical, possession team (Spain, France) vs a physical, fouling team — the fouling team accumulates cards.
4. **Desperate team vs dominant possessor**: The team without the ball fouls to win it. High possession differential → more fouls on the lower-possession team.
5. **High-stakes elimination match**: Teams that must win take more risks, including tactical fouls.

### Specific thresholds (from WC2026 dataset)
| Referee yellow avg | Market stance |
|-------------------|---------------|
| > 4.5 | Over 3.5 cards near-certain |
| 4.0–4.5 | Over 3.5 cards strong; over 2.5 near-certain |
| 3.5–4.0 | Over 2.5 reliable; over 3.5 situational |
| 3.0–3.5 | Over 2.5 only with strong context |
| < 3.0 | Avoid |

## When the Market Performs Poorly

### False indicators (avoid cards)
1. **Both teams with 0 yellows in tournament**: France, Germany, Japan all finished group stage with 0 yellows — even with moderate-high refs, their discipline means cards don't accumulate
2. **Dead rubber** (both eliminated): Reduced motivation = less desperate fouling = fewer cards than the ref's average suggests
3. **Lenient ref (< 3.0 avg)**: Even physical matches stay under 2.5 with a lenient ref
4. **Two low-block teams**: Neither presses high → fewer fouls → fewer cards

## Common Mistakes
- Applying referee average without checking both teams' discipline records
- Assuming "physical match" = more cards without checking ref profile
- Ignoring whether the high-card team is the team with starters already booked

## Player Prop — Most Reliable Profile for Individual Cards
A player is highly likely to be carded when:
- Already booked (1 yellow) + must-win game
- Highest foul count in their team's tournament record
- Plays a pressing/disruptive midfield role
- Their team is desperate (trailing or must-win)

**WC2026 Examples:**
- Ezatolahi (Iran): 1Y, 4 fouls in tournament, must-win MD3 vs Egypt → Marciniak 4.26 → ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Sarpreet Singh (NZ): 1Y, +5.08 On-Off (will be everywhere), must-win vs Belgium → ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Red Card Market
Only back red cards when:
- Referee avg red > 0.20 (confirmed threshold where reds occur in ~1 in 5 games)
- A player with 2+ yellow-card risk (already booked) is playing a physical must-win match
- Late game, trailing team with a player already on a yellow making desperate challenges

**WC2026 Refs at red threshold**: Barton 0.35, Letexier 0.29, Turpin 0.27, Elfath 0.26, Marciniak 0.20
