# BTTS — Both Teams to Score

## When BTTS Yes Performs Well

1. **Both teams must score** (must-win situations for both): Both will attack with urgency → goals more likely on both sides
2. **Both GKs have poor save%** (< 65%): When both keepers concede at above-average rates, both teams scoring is structural
3. **Trailing team's attack is good enough**: If the losing team has a genuine threat (Haaland, Mbappé, Salah), one goal from them is likely once they're chasing
4. **High-volume attack vs open defense**: Norway (6 SoT/game, xG/shot 0.19) vs any team with Nyland (40%) → both score
5. **Late-game desperation opens space**: When a must-win team pushes forward in the final 20 mins, counter-attack goals follow

## When BTTS No Performs Well

1. **Elite GK shuts out one side**: Beiranvand (86.7%) makes Egypt BTTS almost impossible — the 13.3% concede rate means most matches vs Iran end 0-0 or 1-0 for one side
2. **Low-block team generates < 3 SoT/game**: If Iran generates 3.5 SoT against a 77.8% save GK, expected goals for Iran = 0.78 → clean sheet for the opponent ~40% of the time
3. **One-sided dominance**: When France (xGA 0.17/game) faces a weak team, the opponent rarely scores. BTTS No is the lean.
4. **Already-qualified team plays without urgency**: They may keep a clean sheet by defending comfortably while the opponent generates nothing

## Common Mistakes
- Backing BTTS Yes because "both teams attacked" — check GK quality first
- Ignoring motivation: a team that can qualify with a draw won't push for goals they don't need

## The Iran BTTS Pattern
Iran's profile creates a specific BTTS lean:
- Iran concedes ~0.78 expected goals/game (via Shobeir-level GKs, 22% concede rate)
- Iran scores ~0.78 expected goals/game (low block counter)
- **BTTS: slight Yes lean** — both teams score ~0.7-0.8 expected goals → the match is genuinely 50/50 on BTTS
- But with Beiranvand, Iran's opponent's expected goals drop to 0.67 → **BTTS No becomes the lean** vs any team that doesn't generate extraordinary volume
