# Moneyline / Match Result (1X2)

## Probability-First Star Rating System

**Never assign stars first. Calculate probability first. Stars must follow the number.**

| Stars | Implied probability | Use for |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | >70% | Near-certain outcomes only |
| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | 55–70% | Strong, well-supported picks |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | 40–55% | Value picks at decent odds |
| ⭐⭐ | 25–40% | Long-shot value only |

## When a Favourite is Reliable (back the win)

1. **Massive GK quality gap**: Favourite faces a weak GK; opponent faces an elite GK. Model produces >2.0 expected goal differential.
2. **xG regression due**: Favourite has >1.0 xG deficit AND faces a weak GK. Regression will arrive.
3. **Dominant tactical profile**: Spain-vs-Group-E-level opponent where PPDA, possession, and defensive restriction all point one way.
4. **Opponent must open up**: Opponent needs to win → they'll eventually abandon their shape → gaps for the favourite to exploit.

## When Draws Are More Likely (fade the favourite)

1. **Both teams qualify with a draw**: If both teams advance with 1 point, neither has strong motivation to push for a win
2. **Low-block team with elite GK**: Iran-style teams draw constantly — their structure minimizes goals in both directions
3. **Both teams' expected goals 0.8–1.5**: The model says roughly 1 goal each → draw is the single most likely outcome
4. **Technical disparity but GK compensation**: A better team facing a world-class GK (Beiranvand, Courtois level) will be suppressed to 1 goal

## Common Mistakes
- Giving a team ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ because they're clearly the better team without running the model
- Ignoring motivation: a team that can draw its way through will play for the draw
- Ignoring GK quality: the favourite's star rating must account for the opponent's GK, not just the favourite's attack

## WC2026 Observed Errors
- **Egypt win vs Iran (MD3) given ⭐⭐⭐⭐**: Model said Egypt expected goals ~0.67 (suppressed by Beiranvand), Iran ~0.78 (by Shobeir). This pointed to a draw as the single most likely result. The ⭐⭐⭐⭐ should have been ⭐⭐⭐ (draw was the modal outcome). **Result: 1-1 draw — Egypt win missed.**

## Supporting Evidence Framework
A strong moneyline pick requires 3+ of these:
- [ ] GK quality advantage clearly in favourite's favour
- [ ] Expected goals model shows >1.0 gap
- [ ] Opponent motivation analysis confirms they cannot play for draw
- [ ] Tactical profile mismatch (pressing team vs low block = favourite presses, creates more chances)
- [ ] Squad depth advantage for fatigue management
