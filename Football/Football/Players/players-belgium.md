# Belgium — Player Profiles

## Thibaut Courtois (GK)
- **Role**: Starting GK. Belgium's defensive foundation and returning elite.
- **WC2026 stats**: 83.3% save% — among the best in the dataset
- **Key insight**: Courtois returning from injury is the single biggest reason Belgium can compete in this tournament. Belgium with Courtois = 1 GA/game. Their defensive identity runs through him.
- **Discipline**: 0 yellow cards

## Kevin De Bruyne (MF)
- **Role**: Creative engine, central midfield.
- **WC2026 stats**: 9 shots, 14 crosses, 0G through MD2. SoT% 22.2% — his worst return in years. Age 34.
- **Key insight**: De Bruyne is generating volume but not converting. His 0 goals through MD2 contributed to Belgium's -2.14 xG deficit. When his xG regression arrived (MD3, 5-1 NZ), the goals came from others, not him. Monitoring his effectiveness vs elite defences is critical.

## Romelu Lukaku (FW)
- **Role**: Starting striker, MD1-MD2. Tactically limited by defensive opponents.
- **WC2026 stats**: 0 SoT in 97 minutes across MD1-MD2. Scored in MD3 vs Crocombe (54.5%).
- **Key insight**: Lukaku scores against weak GKs (NZ's Crocombe) but generates nothing against organised defences. His MD3 goal flatters him — the opponent was the worst GK in the dataset. Against elite GKs, he is a non-factor.

## Dodi Lukaku / De Cuyper (WB)
- **Role**: Left wing-back. Belgium's most effective attacking wide player.
- **WC2026 stats**: 80% SoT% (dataset record for any outfield player), +2.36 On-Off, 1 yellow card
- **Key insight**: De Cuyper's SoT% record means almost every shot he attempts is on target. His 1 yellow card entering the knockout round is a suspension risk — another booking = miss next match.
