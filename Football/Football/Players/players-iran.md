# Iran — Player Profiles

## Alireza Beiranvand (GK)
- **Role**: Starting GK. Iran's entire competitive viability rests on him.
- **WC2026 stats**: 86.7% save% (dataset record), 15 SoTA in 2 games, -1.03 xGA vs goals conceded
- **Key insight**: Beiranvand saved Iran ~1 goal above expected across the tournament. He is worth approximately 1 goal per game — the difference between Iran drawing and losing. His 86.7% vs the dataset average (~75%) is a ~14 percentage point advantage that translates directly to results. The best individual GK performance in the WC2026 dataset.

## Saman Ghoddos / Mehdi Rezaeian (WB)
- **Role**: Right wing-back. Iran's primary and only attacking outlet.
- **WC2026 stats**: 14 individual crosses (dataset record), 1G from 1 shot
- **Key insight**: Rezaeian is Iran's entire attack. Every counter-attack routes through his right-side delivery. 14 crosses in 2 games is an individual dataset record — he is relentless. His 1G from 1 shot (G/SoT 1.00) shows he can finish when he gets chances.

## Mehdi Taremi (FW)
- **Role**: Central striker / lone target.
- **WC2026 stats**: 0G in 169 minutes, 6 offsides (dataset record)
- **Key insight**: Taremi cannot time his runs. 6 offsides means he is constantly caught by the offside trap — he makes the runs but too early. Iran's counter-attack depends on Rezaeian finding Taremi, but Taremi keeps running himself offside. A frustrated tournament for a technically capable striker.
