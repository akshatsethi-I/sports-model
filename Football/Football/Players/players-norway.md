# Norway — Player Profiles

## Erling Haaland (FW)
- **Role**: Central striker. Norway's entire attacking system exists to serve him.
- **WC2026 stats**: 4G/10sh, G/SoT 0.57 (joint-highest in dataset), SoT% 70%, 0 offsides in 2 games
- **Key insight**: 0 offsides in 2 games is extraordinary for a striker who makes constant runs in behind. His timing is elite. Every cross from Ryerson/Heggem targets Haaland. Norway score when he converts; they don't when he doesn't. Against France's elite CB pair (Saliba-Upamecano), he still scored 1.

## Håkon Heggem (DF/WB)
- **Role**: Left wing-back. Norway's most impactful player outside Haaland.
- **WC2026 stats**: +15.46 On-Off — the highest of any regular starter in the dataset
- **Key insight**: When Heggem plays, Norway are a completely different team on the left side. His delivery and overlapping runs create the cross quality that feeds Haaland. His absence would reduce Norway's attacking output by ~40%.

## Ørjan Nyland (GK)
- **Role**: Starting GK. Norway's fatal vulnerability.
- **WC2026 stats**: 40% save% — concedes 60% of shots on target
- **Key insight**: Nyland is Norway's single biggest weakness. Against France (Mbappé 3.52 SoT/90), a 40% GK was always going to cost them 3-4 goals. Any elite attack will expose him. Norway need to outscore opponents, not defend.

## Martin Ødegaard (MF) — The paradox
- **WC2026 stats**: -7.41 On-Off — Norway's captain makes them measurably worse
- **Key insight**: Ødegaard's technical style conflicts with Norway's direct, physical approach. He slows the build-up when Norway need to go direct to Haaland. A genuine tactical paradox — the team's best individual is a net negative in this system.
