# Belgium

## Overview
WC2026 Group G. **Final standing: 1st (7pts — 2W 1D 0L)**
MD1: 1-1 Egypt (Hany OG) | MD2: 0-0 Iran | MD3: 5-1 New Zealand

The tournament story: the worst attacking luck deficit in the dataset (−2.14 xG vs goals after MD2), resolved in a single explosive night when they beat New Zealand 5-1 and the regression came all at once. Group winners. Now in R32 vs Senegal.

Data pending. Belgium play a 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1 system.

## Preferred Formation
4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1 with heavy use of wide channels and crosses.

## Style of Play

**Build-up structure**: High possession (59.4% over 3 games). 88% pass completion. 227+ final-third passes/game — the highest penetration rate of any team in the dataset outside Spain. 7 through balls in 2 games (3rd in dataset). Belgium circulate and probe rather than play direct.

**Pressing intensity**: PPDA 9.8 — active press. Press start distance 45.0m. 19 high turnovers generating 6 shot-ending sequences (31.58% — dataset record for shot conversion from turnovers). Belgium win the ball high and immediately threaten.

**Defensive shape**: Courtois (GK, 75.0% save% over 3 games) behind a structured back four. xGA 1.94 in 3 games (0.65/game). Opponents generate only 8 SoTA in 3 games (2.67/game) — Belgium's 3-game SoTA-allowed is the lowest in the dataset, though the small sample must be regressed toward mean for modelling. xG/shot allowed: 0.07.

**Transition play**: 52 crosses in 3 games (17.3/game). Primary delivery mechanism is wide — Castagne (right), De Cuyper (left). Cross success 23.1% — above Spain's 18%, below Norway's 26.7%. Set-piece threat via aerial presence (Lukaku, Mechele).

## Strengths
- Courtois is elite — 75.0% save% (3-game WC, regressed to ~71.5% for modelling)
- Press wins lead to immediate danger (31.58% shot-ending turnovers — dataset record)
- Deep squad capable of rotating without quality drop
- Highest final-third pass penetration in dataset outside Spain
- De Cuyper (+3.73 On-Off) and Vanaken (+2.81) are genuine positives off the bench / in the XI

## Weaknesses
- **Tielemans (−29.32 On-Off)** — starting in R32; the worst regular player by On-Off in the entire dataset by a massive margin. Drags every metric Belgium produces
- De Bruyne (−2.10 On-Off) — creative engine but net negative vs this dataset
- Belgium SoT-allowed (2.67/game WC) is a tiny 3-game sample; do not treat as a hard wall — must be regressed
- Castagne, De Cuyper, Lukaku all carrying 1 yellow card each (discipline risk)
- Finishing crisis in MD1-MD2 was resolved only when facing the dataset's worst GK (NZ)

## Game-State Tendencies

**When leading**: Belgium absorb and counter. Courtois is elite at protecting leads. They don't need to press high when ahead.

**When drawing**: Continue building. Possession game, probe for gaps. Will use substitutions to find a winner.

**When trailing**: More direct, higher press, more crosses. Historically this creates space for opponents to counter.

## Set-Piece Tendencies
17.3 crosses/game — primary delivery mechanism. Set-piece corners involve Lukaku and Mechele aerial presence. Belgium won 14 corners in 3 WC games (4.67/game).

## Key Players
