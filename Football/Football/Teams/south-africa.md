# South Africa

## Overview
WC2026. **Qualified for Round of 32. R32 fixture: vs Canada, Jun 28.**
Group record: 1W 1D 1L (2pts minimum — exact group TBC)

South Africa are a physically direct, low-possession side that concede territory willingly and rely on transition speed and set pieces. Their xG underperformance (-0.61) and G/SoT of 0.10 signal a clinical problem. The biggest structural flaw: Williams at 66.7% save% and a defence that concedes 65.79% of shots from inside the box. Two red cards in three games is the most alarming discipline stat of any team in the dataset.

## Preferred Formation
Unknown exactly — plays a compact mid-to-low block. Direct transitions through Maseko and Makgopa. Cross-heavy from Modiba.

## Style of Play

**Build-up structure**: 44.3% possession — they do not dominate the ball. 85.5% pass accuracy, 255 final-third passes/game (low volume). Direct speed 1.43 (fastest in the dataset — extremely direct transitions). Only 3 build-up sequences across 3 games, 0 goals from build-up. 1 goal from 3 direct attacks. SA score from set pieces and individual moments, not patterns.

**Pressing intensity**: PPDA 16.7, press start 38.6m — mid-block tendency leaning toward deep. 14 high turnovers, only 2 shot-ending, 0 goal-ending (14.29% shot conversion from turnovers) — they win the ball back but can't convert the transitions to danger consistently.

**Attacking shape**: 33 shots / 10 SoT (30.3% SoT%) — poor shot selection. xG/shot 0.08 (low quality). Crosses: 27 total, 6 successful (22.2%) — cross delivery is primary mechanism but conversion is poor. 7 through balls — occasional direct balls in behind.

**Defensive shape**: 38 shots conceded, 10 SoT, xG/shot faced 0.09. 65.79% of shots from inside the box — opponents penetrate centrally. Goals conceded -0.38 vs xG (slightly fortunate). 100% of goals conceded from inside the box — no long-range goals conceded, all are from dangerous positions.
