- Isak (30'), Gyökeres (59'), Svanberg (84'), Ayari (7', 90+6'); Rekik (43')

### Matchday 2
**Netherlands 5–1 Sweden** | June 20 | NRG Stadium, Houston | Att: 68,777
- Brobbey (5', 17'), Gakpo (47', 54'), Summerville (89'); Elanga (59')
- **Netherlands destroyed Sweden** — Brobbey brace inside 17 minutes killed the game. Gakpo dominant in second half. Elanga's consolation brief respite. Sweden's defensive fragility exposed.

**Japan 4–0 Tunisia** | June 20 | Estadio BBVA, Guadalupe | Att: 51,243
- Kamada (4'), Ueda (31', 83'), Itō (69')
- Japan clinical and organised. **Kamada again decisive early.** Ueda brace shows Japan have a reliable finisher. Tunisia offered nothing.

## Key observations

- **Netherlands**: **Most impressive team in the group on paper** — 7 goals in 2 games, Brobbey explosive, Gakpo controlling, Summerville dangerous wide. Face eliminated Tunisia in MD3 — almost certain to win and top the group.
- **Japan**: **Tactically excellent** — came from 2 down to draw vs Netherlands, then dismantled Tunisia 4–0. Kamada key: scores early and sets the tone. **Need at least a draw vs Sweden to guarantee 2nd place.**
- **Sweden**: **High ceiling, low floor** — brilliant vs Tunisia (5–1) but absolutely taken apart by Netherlands (1–5). Isak and Gyökeres world-class but defence is a liability. **Must beat Japan to go through as 2nd; could even top the group if they win big and Netherlands slip.**
- **Tunisia**: Eliminated after two heavy defeats. Structurally disorganised, easily pressed.
