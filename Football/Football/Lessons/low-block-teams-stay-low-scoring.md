# Low-Block Teams Create Low-Scoring Matches

## The Lesson
Teams with PPDA > 15 and 0 build-up sequences (Iran profile) create structurally low-scoring matches regardless of the opponent's quality. The under-goals market is systematically undervalued against such teams. The total expected goals model suppresses both sides.

## Evidence

### Iran WC2026
- PPDA 17.0, 0 build-up sequences (unique in dataset)
- Possession 39.2%
- xG/game: 1.06 (lowest in dataset)
- Results: 2-2 NZ (drew), 0-0 Belgium (0-0), 1-1 Egypt (drew)
- Average total goals: **4 goals in 3 games = 1.33/game** → consistently low
- The one match that went over 2.5 (vs NZ 2-2) involved NZ also scoring 2 — NZ's directness was the driver

### The Mechanism
A PPDA 17.0 team forces:
1. Opponent to have 60%+ possession = more opponent build-up = more shots BUT from low quality positions (opponent forced to probe, not penetrate)
2. Iran's counter = 3.5 SoT/game (low volume), 86.7% GK save% (low conversion allowed)
3. Result: ~0.67-0.78 expected goals per side → under 2.5 is the base expectation

## Confidence
**High** — confirmed across all 3 Iran group-stage matches.

## How to Apply
1. If a team has PPDA > 14 and 0 build-up sequences: back Under 2.5 unless:
   - Their GK is poor (save% < 65%)
   - The opponent has an elite striker (Mbappé, Haaland) who will beat the GK's xGA
2. The under-goals market is systematically strongest when a low-block team is desperate (must-win) because they'll push forward AND their structure degrades → counter-attack goals both ways
3. If the low-block team concedes first, they must press forward → the match opens up → consider Over after a goal

## Counterexamples
- If the high-press team is Belgium or Spain (70%+ possession) vs Iran: the high volume of speculative shots eventually produces goals from chance quality alone, even at low conversion
