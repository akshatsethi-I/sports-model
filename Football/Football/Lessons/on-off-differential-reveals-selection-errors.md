# On-Off Differential Reveals Selection Errors

## The Lesson
On-Off differential is the most actionable team intelligence metric from FBRef. It directly tells you which players make the team better or worse — and by how much. Large On-Off gaps (>3.0 in either direction) identify selection errors that should inform predictions.

## Evidence from WC2026

### Egypt: Trézéguet vs Marmoush
| Player | Mins | Goals | On-Off |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| Trézéguet | ~15 | 1 | **+5.45** |
| Marmoush | ~165 | 0 | **-5.45** |

**The largest individual On-Off contrast in the WC2026 dataset.** Egypt's manager started Marmoush and brought Trézéguet on as a sub. The data is unambiguous: every minute Marmoush starts over Trézéguet, Egypt lose attacking value.

### Norway: Ødegaard
- Ødegaard: 170 mins, On-Off **-7.41** — Norway's captain and supposed star makes the team significantly worse
- Heggem: 173 mins, On-Off **+15.46** — the player Norway didn't know was their best

### Belgium: De Cuyper vs Onana
- De Cuyper: +2.36 — Belgium's left wing-back is their most impactful outfield player
- Onana: -2.36 — the central midfielder makes Belgium worse

## Confidence
**High** — confirmed across multiple teams; backed by football logic (Trézéguet's G/SoT 1.00 explains the +5.45).

## How to Apply
1. When reading FBRef On-Off data, flag any player with |On-Off| > 3.0
2. If a flagged player is starting despite being negative, the team has a selection error — their actual output will be lower than their best-case data
3. If a positive impact player is being underused as a sub, back them as a scorer prop (they'll likely enter late)
4. In predictions, always note which lineup gives the team their best On-Off profile

## Limitations
- Small sample size (< 45 mins) makes On-Off noisy — treat with caution
- 180-min starters have no "off" time, so their On-Off reflects team average — less informative
- Does not control for opponent quality faced during on/off windows
