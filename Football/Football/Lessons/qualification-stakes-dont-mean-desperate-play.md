# Qualification Stakes ≠ Desperate Play

## The Lesson
In a 48-team World Cup with 8 best 3rd-place teams qualifying, a **draw often qualifies a team** — even when framing suggests they "must win." Always check the current 3rd-place standings before assessing match motivation. Do not assume conservative play from a team with 3 points — they may still play to win for seeding or goal difference.

## Evidence

### WC2026 Session Error
Initial analysis of Belgium vs NZ and Iran vs Egypt framed both as "must win" for the trailing teams. User correction: with 8 best 3rd-place teams qualifying, 2pts + a draw = 3pts = likely qualification for Belgium and Iran.

The 3rd-place cut-off from the screenshot: ~4pts (goal difference tiebreaker). This meant Iran (2pts) needed a win — their motivation was real. But the initial framing was wrong without checking the table.

### The Corrected Framework
Always check:
1. What does the team need? Win / draw / any result?
2. Does a draw qualify via top-2 OR via 3rd-place table?
3. What is the current 3rd-place cut-off (points + GD)?
4. Does the simultaneous match in the group affect required result?
5. Even if a team *can* afford a draw, do they play to win for seeding? (Often yes.)

## Confidence
**High** — This is a structural feature of the 48-team World Cup format. Will apply to every group-stage match in this and future 48-team tournaments.

## How to Apply
Before every PREDICT: run the Pre-Prediction Checklist Step 1 from CLAUDE.md. Map all qualification scenarios for both teams before assessing playing style.

## Counterexamples
- Teams already eliminated genuinely have no motivation — motivation analysis differs for them (some rest players, some play with freedom)
- Teams already qualified (top-2 locked) may rotate — check confirmed qualification before the match
