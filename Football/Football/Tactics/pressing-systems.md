# Pressing Systems

## Overview
A pressing system defines *where, when, and how* a team tries to win the ball back without it.

Three key variables:
- **Height**: where on the pitch the press is triggered
- **Trigger**: what event initiates the press (back-pass, GK ball, specific pass)
- **Orientation**: man-oriented (follow runners) vs zone-oriented (hold shape, press the ball)

---

## High Press (Gegenpressing)
PPDA typically < 8. Compact shape that pushes high as a unit. Win the ball within 5–8 seconds of losing it.

**Strengths**: Forces errors in dangerous areas → high xG. Prevents opponents building rhythm. Short, fast transitions.

**Weaknesses**: Physically demanding — degrades in the 60–75 min window. Vulnerable to teams that can play through the press. A single bad trigger leaves space in behind.

**WC2026 examples**: Spain (PPDA 7.5, press start 51.8m — dataset record), USA (PPDA 7.1)

---

## Mid-Block
PPDA typically 9–12. Two compact lines of four around the halfway line. Press when opposition enters your half or makes a poor touch.

**Strengths**: More physically sustainable. Protects against through-balls. Works well against technically superior opposition.

**Weaknesses**: Cedes possession and territory. Relies on quality of counter-attack to generate xG.

**WC2026 examples**: Belgium (9.4), Uruguay (10.1), Egypt (11.1)

---

## Low Block
PPDA > 12, often significantly. 9–10 players behind the ball at all times.

**Strengths**: Hard to break down against teams lacking width or creativity. Frustrates opponents. Effective for teams with a physical striker to hold up play.

**Weaknesses**: Concedes xG volume over time. Under sustained pressure, small errors lead to shots. Limited xG generation — relies on set pieces or long balls.

**WC2026 examples**: Iran (PPDA 17.0, 0 build-up sequences, counter-press only)

---

## Man-Oriented vs Zone-Oriented

| | Man-Oriented | Zone-Oriented |
|---|---|---|
| **Logic** | Follow the runner | Hold your zone, press the ball-carrier |
| **Risk** | Pulled out of position by decoys | Gaps if ball moves faster than press |
| **Example** | RB Leipzig | Guardiola's City |

---

## Press Resistance
A team's ability to play through or around a press.
- **GK distribution**: short (risky but maintains possession) vs long (safe but surrenders territory)
- **CB splits**: wide positioning to stretch press triggers
- **Line-breaking passes**: vertical passes that bypass a press line in one move

---

## How it affects betting
- A high-press team (Spain, USA) vs a low-block team (Iran) = low xG for the low-block team on the counter; high xG volume but moderate quality for the press team
- Low-block teams are disciplined card-wise (they absorb rather than press); their opponents' cards come from frustration
- When a high-press team's press degrades (60–75 min), their opponents' best scoring window opens
