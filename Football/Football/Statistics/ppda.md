# PPDA — Passes Per Defensive Action

## Definition
`PPDA = opponent passes in their own 60% of pitch ÷ defensive actions in that zone`

**Lower PPDA = more aggressive press.** A PPDA of 7 means the pressing team allows only 7 passes before winning the ball back.

## Benchmarks
| PPDA | Press style |
|------|-------------|
| < 7  | Elite high press |
| 7–9  | Active press |
| 9–12 | Moderate mid-block |
| > 12 | Deep defensive block |

## Why it matters for match flow
- **High-press teams (low PPDA)** force turnovers high up → shorter, more intense transitions → higher xG for both teams → volatile matches
- **Low-press teams (high PPDA)** absorb and counter → lower overall xG → tight, low-scoring matches
- When a **high-press team plays a high-press team**, the midfield is physical and one early goal can collapse the loser's structure

## PPDA vs OPPDA
- **PPDA** = how hard your team presses
- **OPPDA** = how hard the opposition presses against you (press resistance metric)
- A team with low PPDA but high OPPDA is pressing well but struggling under the opponent's press

## Limitations
- PPDA doesn't capture press coordination or triggering quality
- Inflated in matches against weak opposition (more passes to defend)
- Best interpreted alongside xG and possession/territory metrics

## How it affects betting
- Iran (PPDA 17.0) vs any active press team: expect a low-scoring match with Iran sitting back and countering
- Spain/USA (PPDA 7-7.5) pressing high: expect volatile early phases, more cards from frustrated opponents
- Teams with PPDA > 13 in must-win situations: structural mismatch — they will foul more as they can't win the ball cleanly
