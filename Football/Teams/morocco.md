# Morocco

## Overview
WC2026. 5-game record: **W3 D2 L0**, 10 goals scored, 4 conceded. Advanced through group + R32 + R16. Two draws suggest Morocco are capable of absorbing pressure without losing but also struggle to convert vs quality opposition.

**Key insight:** Morocco are xG-efficient (8.32 xG from 5G = 1.66/game, 10 goals scored = slight overperformance of +1.68). Their attacking quality is real. Their biggest threat is counter-attack: **3 fast-break goals from 10 attempts (30%)** — the highest fast-break conversion rate of any QF team.

## Manager
Walid Regragui. Passive-block system. PPDA 14.8 — sit deep, absorb pressure, counter-attack. Morocco's press start is deep (41.4m). They rely on shape and recovery rather than winning the ball high up.

## Preferred Formation
4-4-2 / 4-5-1 defensive block. Transitions via fast breaks and Hakimi's overlapping runs.

## Style of Play

**Build-up:** 60.4% possession (high for a counter-attacking team — indicates they've had good game states). 88.1% pass accuracy. 4.82 passes/sequence. NOT a direct team — sequences are patient, but attacking transitions are rapid (Direct Speed 1.54).

**Pressing:** PPDA 14.8 — one of the most passive pressers in the QF bracket. They sit compact and absorb. Only 52 pressed sequences in 5 games.

**Attacking patterns:** Fast breaks are their primary weapon (10 attempts, 3 goals — 30% conversion). Also dangerous from counter-attack through Hakimi's runs and Rahimi off the bench. 113 box touches in 5G (22.6/game).

**Defensive shape:** 25.2 clearances/game — elite deep-block defensive volume. Only 2.4 SoT conceded/game. SP defense is exceptional: **0 goals conceded from 19 SP shots** (5G). Opponents allowed only 20.2 box touches/game. Aerial weakness: **46.7% aerial duels won** (below average) — significant vulnerability vs France's aerial dominance (64.7%).

## Key Players

- **Achraf Hakimi** — DF, 1G/13sh/4SoT. 30 crosses in 5G (6/game). Primary ball carrier and overlap threat.
- **Ismael Saibari** — FW, 3G/10sh/3SoT. SoT/90: 0.71.
- **Soufiane Rahimi** — FW (sub), 2G/6sh/4SoT. SoT/90: 2.69 — elite substitute threat.
- **Azzedine Ounahi** — MF, 2G/4sh/2SoT. On-Off: +1.04.
- **Bilal El Khannouss** — MF, 0G/6sh/4SoT. SoT% 66.7% — high efficiency in shots.
- **Yassine Bounou** — GK. Save%: **69.2%** (9 saves / 13 SoTA, 5G). CS: 2. Bayesian-regressed to 69.5%.
- **Sofyan Amrabat** — MF. Defensive anchor. On-Off: +3.05 in limited games.

## Strengths
- Deep defensive block: 25.2 clearances/game, 0 SP goals conceded (5G)
- Fast-break conversion: 3 goals from 10 attempts (30%)
- Hakimi attacking overlap: 30 crosses in 5G
- PPDA suppression: opponents only get 2.4 SoT/game vs Morocco's block
- Super-sub threat: Rahimi (2.69 SoT/90) and Ounahi off bench

## Weaknesses
- **Aerial duels: 46.7% won** — major vulnerability vs France's 64.7%
- PPDA 14.8 — completely passive; France's possession will dominate
- No set-piece goals from 18 SP shots (5G) — poor SP conversion (0.75 xG generated, 2 goals)
- SoT/game (att): only 5.0 — limited volume vs quality defenses
- Neil El Aynaoui On-Off: -10.30 at 5G — near-constant starter, structural drag
