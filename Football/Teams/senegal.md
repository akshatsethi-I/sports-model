# Senegal

## Overview
WC2026 Group I. **Final standing: 2nd (6pts — 2W 0D 1L)**
MD1: lost | MD2: won | MD3: 5-0 Iraq

Senegal recovered from a poor start to win 5-0 vs Iraq in MD3 and qualify 2nd in Group I ahead of Norway on goal difference. The 5-0 scoreline is misleading — it came against the weakest team in the group. Their underlying data through MD1-MD2 showed serious structural issues that 5-0 vs Iraq does not fully resolve.

**R32 vs Belgium (2026-07-01).** Critical lineup changes: Mory Diaw starts in goal (Mendy benched), Koulibaly does NOT start (Seck replaces him), Iliman Ndiaye starts. These three changes collectively improve the Senegal lineup significantly vs their MD1-MD2 baseline.

## Style of Play

**Build-up structure**: ~58% possession (3-game incl. Iraq), 88% pass completion. PPDA 15.7 over 3 games (was 20.8 MD1-MD2 — Iraq slightly improved the figure). Cross-heavy: 63 crosses in 3 games (21/game). Ismaila Sarr and Iliman Ndiaye are the primary direct-attack mechanisms.

**Pressing intensity**: PPDA 15.7 — deep passive block. Press start ~40m. 16+ high turnovers but historically poor at converting them into goals in MD1-MD2. Iraq showed they CAN score in volume when opposition is weak.

**Defensive shape**: xG/shot faced 0.14 (3-game, including easy Iraq shots). Mendy (57.1% save%) pulled mid-game in MD2. Now replaced by Diaw for R32. Koulibaly (−7.19 On-Off) NOT starting — replaced by Seck (+3.16), a net +10.35 On-Off swing in Senegal's defensive quality.

**Transition play**: Sarr is the primary direct outlet. Iliman Ndiaye (+6.82 On-Off) starting is a major upgrade. Jackson (−2.81 On-Off) does NOT start — positive change. Pape Gueye (−1.78 On-Off) in the XI.

## Strengths
- **Ismaila Sarr**: dangerous wide attacker, pace, direct running
- **Iliman Ndiaye (+6.82 On-Off)**: best On-Off in the starting XI; starts R32
- **Koulibaly OUT → Seck (+3.16) IN**: net +10.35 On-Off swing, Senegal's defence materially better
- **Mory Diaw starts**: upgrade over Mendy based on On-Off (though save% data is 1 SoTA vs Iraq = noise)

- **Lamine Camara**: box-to-box dynamism in CDM partnership
- Corners: 20 won in 3 WC games (6.67/game) — high corner rate

## Weaknesses

- **Mory Diaw save% unknown** in competitive football: 100% vs Iraq = 1 SoT, pure noise. Real save% is uncertain — model uses 60% (squad/regression average)
- **Pape Gueye (−1.78 On-Off)** starts in XI

- Jackson (−2.81) NOT starting is positive, but Mané (0G, negative goal contribution this tournament) starts at CF
- Offsides: 8 in 3 games (2.67/game) — poor off-the-ball discipline

## Game-State Tendencies
**When leading**: Deep block. PPDA becomes even more extreme. Dangerous on the counter via Sarr/Ndiaye.
**When drawing**: Cross-heavy, direct attacks through Sarr. Build-up is limited (PPDA 15.7 = they don't commit many to press).
**When trailing**: Historically poor in MD1-MD2 (0pts before Iraq). Open up; become more vulnerable on the break.

## Set-Piece Tendencies
63 crosses in 3 games (21/game). 20 corners won (6.67/game). Set pieces are a primary route to goal for Senegal in tight defensive matches.

## Historical Lessons
- Koulibaly (−7.19 On-Off) at 35: his pace liability was Senegal's biggest defensive problem through MD1-MD2. R32 lineup without him is a structural upgrade, not a downgrade.
- Mendy (57.1% save%) was the weakest regular GK in the dataset after Nyland. Diaw is an upgrade on On-Off grounds even if his actual save% is unknown.
- Senegal's xG overperformance (+2.86) is largely Iraq-driven. Do not treat this as signal for R32.
- BTTS: Senegal's Footystats BTTS% = 40% (10 games). But this includes strong defensive opponents; vs Belgium's offensive machine, the BTTS Yes model output (64%) is more reliable.
